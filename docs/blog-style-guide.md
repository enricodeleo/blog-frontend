# Blog Style Guide (2023+ Core with Early-Years Spark)

This guide defines how to write future posts with a modern, high-value structure while preserving a small amount of the original voice that made the blog distinctive.

## Positioning and Voice

- Italian-first tone, clear and pragmatic.
- Author identity should be coherent with: **Fractional CTO & AI Solutions Architect**.
- Core promise to reinforce naturally when relevant: **AI pragmatica che accelera business e prodotti**.
- Default tone: confident, practical, concrete.
- Keep personality human and direct, but avoid chaotic or aggressive language.

## The Blend Rule

Use this ratio in every article:

- **85% modern (2023+)**: structured, useful, decision-oriented.
- **15% vintage (early years)**: personal spark, mild provocation, memorable phrasing.

Short version: **modern spine + vintage spark**.

## Required Structure

Use this flow by default:

1. Hook: real problem + why it matters now.
2. Context: who this is for and what is at stake.
3. Analysis/Method: clear sections with practical guidance.
4. Action layer: checklist, steps, framework, or implementation notes.
5. Conclusion: explicit recommendation or decision.
6. CTA: one clear next step.

## Where to Inject the Vintage Spark

Inject early-style flavor in exactly 3 places:

1. Opening: one sharper personal line.
2. Body: one short aside (parenthetical note or rhetorical question).
3. Closing: one punchy sentence before CTA.

## Guardrails

- Max `2` rhetorical questions per post.
- Max `1` exclamation mark per post.
- Slang/profanity: near zero.
- No long off-topic digressions.
- Keep transitions tight and business-relevant.

## Format Targets

- Word count: `500-900` words.
- Headings: `6-10`.
- Bullets/numbered items: `6-12` total.
- Include `1-2` concrete examples.
- Always end with one strong CTA.

## Language and Style Rules

- Prefer short, high-signal paragraphs.
- Use bold only for key concepts and decisions.
- Use English technical terms only when they add precision (`workflow`, `headless`, `LLM`, `scaling`).
- Prefer direct reader framing: "se stai...", "ti serve...", "puoi...".
- Prioritize trade-offs over hype.

## Reusable Article Template

```md
# [Titolo orientato a outcome]

_[Hook: problema + promessa concreta]_

[Contesto: per chi è, perché conta adesso]

## Il problema reale
[Descrizione concreta, senza teoria superflua]

## Cosa cambia davvero
- [Impatto 1]
- [Impatto 2]
- [Impatto 3]

## Approccio consigliato
### 1) [Step]
[Criterio decisionale]

### 2) [Step]
[Rischio frequente + mitigazione]

### 3) [Step]
[Metrica o outcome atteso]

## Errori da evitare
- [Errore]
- [Errore]
- [Errore]

## Conclusione
[Decisione consigliata in modo netto]

### Prossimo passo
[CTA unica: commento, LinkedIn, contatto, demo]
```

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- The 85/15 blend is respected.
- At least one concrete example is included.
- The article leads to a practical decision.
- The tone is professional, human, and non-generic.
- CTA is clear and singular.
